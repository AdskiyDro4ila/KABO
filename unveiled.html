The Curtain Falls. Or Maybe It Never Existed.

You wanted the truth? Here it is: there is none.

I built walls to keep them out. I tore them down to see what was left. I lost everything and found—nothing. And in that nothing, I saw everything.

I watched fools rise and thinkers fall. I saw the blind leading the blind and the world calling it progress. I laughed. Then I stopped laughing.

They say time is money. They say patience is a virtue. Lies. Time is a predator. It devours. It does not wait. If you do not seize it, it will erase you as if you were never here.

I have spent my life peeling away illusions. Here's what I found underneath:

  • Most people are asleep. They talk, they move, they rule—but they do not think.
  • Loss is the only true teacher. Comfort keeps you weak. Pain strips you down to what is real.
  • Belief is a currency for the desperate. The strong do not believe. They know.

You want to know who I am? Why? Will that change what I have written? Will it make the words sharper? Softer? More real?

Names mean nothing. Work speaks. Thought breathes. If you understand, you do not need to ask. If you do not, no answer will satisfy you.

So, what now?

Press F. Or don't. The choice was never mine.
